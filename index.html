2)A simply colormap for plotting topo-bathymetric data in matlab

There are a lot of nice built in and readily available colormaps for Matlab (I personally love what the creators of CMOCAEN have done), but as someone who continuously plots coastal elevation data, I found myself desiring a simple and flexible way to differentiate the colormap associated with elevations above the water surface from those below, particularly as water level varies and is non zero.â€™
There were a few options available online, but none were quite meeting my needs perfectly, so I developed my own using some of the existing ones as a starting block. Also, when not TOO burdensome, I actually like to 'recreate the wheel' when it comes to coding. It is nice to know exactly what is happening and have full control over the results. Here is an example of the colormap used in a p-color plot as well as a 3D surface plot.

The colormap can be applied as a function which I call 'cm_split' and the code and a script to recreate the example shown above is included here. Instructions included within the function and example should (hopefully) be self explanatory.

1)Data Visualization

If you are anything like me, these equations may not speak volumes to you, even if you do understand the math.

Some skilled people have an incredible mathematical intuition such that these and other equations reveal physical processes in a deep and informative way, I do not. I am a visual learner and so I turn complex data and equations into something that makes better sense to my way of thinking.
I have discretized the shallow water equations (SWE - shown above) so that they can be applied over a span of time and space as a model. The video below is an example simulation of this simple model where a few different wave components are created at one border and then propagate over a sloping bottom, all according to the SWE. Seeing this connection between the equations and the behavior helps me to better understand what these equations mean and what their application (and limitations) may be

The view can be viewed in high quality (1080p) through the gear icon. The arrows show the relative magnitude and direction of the depth integrated water velocity. It is neat to note how water first moves backwards and then forwards as a wave propegates by. Have you ever felt this while being in the water at a beach?

My version of the Matlab code for this model (which is by no means perfect, efficient or pretty) is available here: SWEmodel
